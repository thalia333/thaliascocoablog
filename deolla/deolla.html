CAFE DE OLLA

3388 19th St, San Francisco, CA 94110

I was really excited to find Café de Olla because I had never tried Oaxacan hot chocolate before and figured it would be the perfect way to round out my series of tasters. Since the cafe is on a side street off of Mission, it is easy to miss if you aren’t looking for it. The space i s small and brightly lit, with colorful tablecloths, art, and paper flags strung on the ceiling. They have nice window seating at the bar and larger tables to sit at and share some words and food.

They serve a variety of drinks and food such as traditional tamales and pozole, and my drink cost $7.50. After I ordered, the barista came to my window table with a clay jug filled with cocoa and a wooden whisk. She showed me how she was beating the hot chocolate until it was frothing and well-mixed before pouring it into an adorable clay mug that matched the vibe of the whole cafe.

It was really cool to get to see Oaxacan hot cocoa being prepared! It added more to the experience, like I wasn’t just drinking something but was witnessing and participating in it being made. The hot chocolate was so delicious, and tasted like cocoa mixed with marshmallows. It wasn’t that thick, with more of a milky taste and made with a less bitter chocolate, instead being sweeter and tasting cozy, closer to a regular hot chocolate.

8/10 Taste



8/10 Price



9/10 Texture



8/10 Dining Experience
